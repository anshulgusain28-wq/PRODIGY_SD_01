Conversion Formulas

Celsius → Fahrenheit
(°C × 9/5) + 32
e.g. 100°C ➔ 212°F
Fahrenheit → Celsius
(°F − 32) × 5/9
e.g. 98.6°F ➔ 37°C
Celsius → Kelvin
°C + 273.15
e.g. 0°C ➔ 273.15 K

About the Scales

■

Celsius °C

The most widely used scale globally. Based on the freezing (0°) and boiling (100°) points of water at standard pressure.

Proposed by Anders Celsius in 1742.
■

Fahrenheit °F

Primarily used in the United States. Water freezes at 32° and boils at 212° on this scale.

Introduced by Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit in 1724.
■

Kelvin K

The SI base unit of temperature. Starts at absolute zero — the coldest theoretically possible temperature.

Defined by Lord Kelvin (William Thomson) in 1848.
